## Foundation Ranges and Fit

This file defines the current working ranges for the foundation layer of the
repository.

The repository has already established that the present longevity base is:

- exercise
- high-quality dietary patterning
- sleep and recovery treatment logic

What this file adds is the next necessary layer:

what counts as being meaningfully in range inside those domains for a real
person.

This is not a one-size-fits-all clinical protocol.
It is a source-anchored working foundation range for the repository.

## Core Position

Naming the foundation is not enough.

A person can say:

- I exercise
- I eat healthy
- I sleep okay

and still not actually be inside a meaningful base.

This file defines the current working range more concretely.

The foundation should now be understood as:

- specific enough to be real
- strong enough to matter
- gentle enough to be sustainable
- matched to recovery capacity
- judged by function, not by label

## Truth Status

### KNOWN

Current physical-activity guidance for adults supports at least 150 to 300
minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity, or 75 to 150 minutes
per week of vigorous-intensity activity, or an equivalent combination. Adults
should also do muscle-strengthening work involving major muscle groups on 2 or
more days per week. Older adults should also include multicomponent physical
activity emphasizing balance and strength. 

Current U.S. dietary guidance emphasizes a healthy dietary pattern across life,
meeting food-group needs with nutrient-dense foods, and limiting added sugars
to less than 10% of calories, saturated fat to less than 10% of calories, and
sodium to less than 2,300 mg per day. It also explicitly emphasizes that the
pattern of eating over time matters more than isolated “healthy choices.” 

Common adult sleep-duration guidance targets 7 to 9 hours for most adults, with
7 to 8 hours often used as the recommendation range for older adults. Recent
sleep-health research in older adults also suggests that greater night-to-night
sleep-efficiency variability is associated with worse cognitive performance,
which supports treating regularity as part of the base rather than only total
hours. 

### WORKING REPOSITORY INFERENCE

For this repository, the foundational base should be treated as a **range-based
floor**, not as an idealized maximum.

That means the right question is not:

What is the most aggressive version of exercise, diet, or sleep?

The better question is:

What range is strong enough to produce real benefit without immediately
destabilizing recovery, adherence, or fit?

## 1. Exercise | Foundational Working Range

### Source-anchored floor

The current source-anchored floor for most adults is:

- 150 to 300 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity
- or 75 to 150 minutes per week of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity
- or a combination of the two
- plus strength training involving all major muscle groups on at least 2 days
  per week

For older adults, or adults with fall risk or lower reserve, balance-oriented
multicomponent activity should also be included. 

### Working repository range

A practical foundational exercise range for the repo is:

- **Aerobic movement:** 150 to 300 minutes per week total
- **Strength training:** 2 to 3 days per week
- **Mobility and balance work:** 2 to 3 days per week, and closer to 3 if the
  person is older, deconditioned, or visibly unstable
- **Sedentary floor rule:** move more and sit less across the day rather than
  treating workouts as compensation for otherwise static living

This is not meant to describe elite training.
It is meant to define the minimum range where the base becomes real. 

### What “in range” looks like for exercise

Exercise is more likely to be in range when it is:

- regular enough to count as structure
- improving or preserving strength, stamina, mobility, or reserve
- recoverable
- not producing repeated crash patterns

Exercise is less likely to be in range when it is:

- too weak to produce any real functional gain
- too aggressive to recover from
- inconsistent enough to be mostly symbolic
- repeatedly worsening sleep, fatigue, pain, or resilience

### Practical interpretation

For the repository, “exercise” does **not** mean:

- random activity when convenient
- punishing intensity by default
- cardio only
- strength only

It means a stable base with enough aerobic work to matter, enough strength work
to preserve muscle and reserve, and enough balance or mobility work to keep the
system functional.

## 2. High-Quality Dietary Patterning | Foundational Working Range

### Source-anchored floor

The current official dietary floor is not one branded diet.
It is a healthy dietary pattern built around nutrient-dense foods, food-group
coverage, and quantitative limits on added sugar, saturated fat, and sodium. 

The most important numerical anchors currently available from the U.S.
guidelines are:

- **Added sugars:** less than 10% of total daily calories
- **Saturated fat:** less than 10% of total daily calories
- **Sodium:** less than 2,300 mg per day

The pattern should also be built from nutrient-dense foods and judged across
time, not by isolated meals. 

### Working repository range

For this repository, the foundational diet range should be treated as:

- **Pattern-first, not label-first**
- **Nutrient-dense, mostly whole-food, and sustainable**
- **Plant-forward in structure**, without requiring strict ideological purity
- **Low enough in added sugar, saturated fat, and sodium to stay inside the
  guideline limits**
- **Supportive of energy, recovery, and adherence**

In practice, that means the foundation should usually look more like:

- vegetables and fruit appearing daily and consistently
- legumes, nuts, seeds, and whole grains playing a meaningful role
- adequate protein distributed across the day rather than crammed into one meal
- unsaturated-fat sources being more central than highly processed fat/sugar
  combinations
- ultra-processed foods not dominating the pattern

This is closer to a Mediterranean-, DASH-, or AHEI-shaped pattern than to a
single rigid diet identity, but the repo does not need to lock one brand name
to define the base yet. The stronger point is pattern quality. 

### What “in range” looks like for diet

Dietary patterning is more likely to be in range when:

- it is stable enough to be real
- it improves or supports energy and recovery
- it is lower-burden than the person’s prior pattern
- it is not so rigid that it breaks adherence
- it is not underfueling the person relative to activity and recovery demands

Dietary patterning is less likely to be in range when:

- “healthy” is too vague to mean anything
- the pattern is mostly reactive restriction
- the person cannot maintain it in real life
- the person is repeatedly under-recovered, underfed, or oscillating between
  extremes
- the diet label matters more than the organismal result

### Practical interpretation

For the repository, “diet” does **not** mean:

- one named camp automatically wins
- calorie austerity equals longevity
- purity equals quality

It means a durable eating pattern that is clearly higher quality than the
likely alternative and is actually helping the body hold.

## 3. Sleep and Recovery | Foundational Working Range

### Source-anchored floor

The most usable starting range for adults is:

- **Adults 18 to 64:** 7 to 9 hours of sleep opportunity per night
- **Older adults 65+:** often 7 to 8 hours as the practical target range

Recent evidence in older adults also supports treating sleep **regularity** and
night-to-night stability as meaningful, not optional. 

### Working repository range

For this repository, the foundational sleep/recovery range should be treated
as:

- **Sleep opportunity:** 7 to 9 hours most nights
- **Regularity:** a fairly stable sleep-wake schedule across the week
- **Recovery reality:** enough restoration to support exercise and normal
  cognitive functioning
- **Escalation rule:** if the person is chronically nonrestorative, repeatedly
  exhausted, or showing signs of insomnia or sleep-disordered breathing, this
  is no longer only a lifestyle issue — it becomes treatment logic

A practical working target for schedule consistency in the repo is to keep
bedtime and wake time from drifting wildly across the week. This is a repo
inference based on the importance of regularity and variability rather than a
formal universal cutpoint. 

### What “in range” looks like for sleep and recovery

Sleep/recovery is more likely to be in range when:

- daytime functioning is better
- recovery from activity is possible
- sleep is regular enough to be real structure
- the person is not living in chronic sleep debt
- the pattern feels restorative rather than merely sedating

Sleep/recovery is less likely to be in range when:

- sleep opportunity is routinely below 7 hours
- sleep is chronically fragmented or nonrestorative
- the person relies on exhaustion rather than recovery
- sleep disorder signals are being ignored
- schedule variability is large enough that restoration never stabilizes

### Practical interpretation

For the repository, “sleep” does **not** mean:

- time in bed alone
- sedation alone
- “I can survive on less” mythology

It means enough consistent restoration to support the rest of the base.

## 4. What the Whole Base Looks Like When It Is Actually In Range

A person is more likely inside the foundational range when all three domains are
present at once in a way the organism can hold.

That usually means:

- exercise is regular and recoverable
- food quality is real and durable
- sleep is sufficient and restorative enough to support the other two

The foundation is not in range if one pillar is being built by destroying the
others.

Examples:

- training hard while sleep is chronically broken
- eating “clean” while underfueling and under-recovering
- sleeping longer while never building real physical capacity

The base is meant to stabilize the organism, not fragment it.

## 5. Minimum Viable Base Versus Stronger Base

### Minimum viable foundation

A person has a minimum viable base when:

- exercise is real enough to count
- diet quality is real enough to count
- sleep/recovery is real enough to count
- none of the three are obviously destabilizing the whole system

This is the floor.

### Stronger foundational base

A person has a stronger base when the same three domains are not only present,
but are visibly improving:

- function
- resilience
- recovery
- day-to-day capacity
- ability to hold the routine over time

This is important because the repository should distinguish between:
a base that exists and a base that is clearly working.

## 6. What This Means for the Repo

So yes — the repo now needs to say something more concrete than:

exercise, diet, sleep

The clearer working statement is:

**A foundational base protocol means:**

- **Exercise:** 150 to 300 minutes per week of moderate activity or 75 to 150
  minutes vigorous, plus 2 to 3 days per week of full-body strength work, with
  balance/mobility added as needed
- **Diet:** a durable, nutrient-dense, mostly whole-food pattern that stays
  under 10% added sugar, under 10% saturated fat, and under 2,300 mg sodium,
  while supporting energy and recovery
- **Sleep:** a real sleep opportunity of about 7 to 9 hours most nights, with
  enough regularity and recovery quality to support the other two

That is the clearest current working foundation range the repository can defend
right now.
